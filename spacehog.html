Spacehog's "Resident Alien": A Collector's Treasure Unearthed

Link to No Code Album on YouTube

In the annals of alternative rock, certain albums shine like hidden gems, patiently waiting for their moment in the spotlight. Among these, Spacehog's "Resident Alien" stands as a quintessential example. Released in 1995, the album, adorned with its whimsical cover art and brimming with eclectic sounds, embodies the spirit of a band poised for greatness. However, it wasn't until years later that "Resident Alien" gained recognition as a true collector's item. In this exploration, we'll unearth the reasons behind the album's belated acclaim and dissect the elements that make it a cherished treasure for music enthusiasts worldwide.

The Genesis of "Resident Alien"

Spacehog, a British alternative rock band formed in New York City, burst onto the scene with an exuberant energy that was both reminiscent of glam rock's heyday and intrinsically tied to the mid-'90s alternative rock explosion. "Resident Alien" served as their inaugural opus, a spirited collection of songs that resonated with a generation hungry for a new musical experience. From the infectious hooks of "In the Meantime" to the introspective musings of "Space Is the Place," the album showcased the band's diverse musical influences and their uncanny ability to marry intricate musicianship with catchy, radio-friendly melodies.

The Collector's Journey Begins While "Resident Alien" received critical acclaim upon its release, it didn't initially achieve the commercial success that some of its contemporaries enjoyed. Yet, this relative obscurity was the beginning of what would become a celebrated journey for collectors and music enthusiasts. As the years passed, the album took on an almost mythical quality, gaining a dedicated following among those in the know. Its scarcity on vinyl and the allure of its limited releases on colored vinyl and picture discs contributed to its mystique, turning it into a sought-after gem for collectors. The Musical Alchemy of "Resident Alien" At the core of "Resident Alien's" enduring appeal lies its exceptional musicality. The band's tight-knit chemistry is palpable, with Royston Langdon's distinctive vocals soaring over Richard Steel's inventive guitar work and the dynamic rhythm section provided by Jonny Cragg and Antony Langdon. The album deftly weaves together elements of glam, punk, and alternative rock, creating a sound that is simultaneously nostalgic and refreshingly original. Songs like "In the Meantime" and "Cruel to Be Kind" showcase the band's ability to craft infectious hooks and memorable choruses, while deeper cuts like "Starside" and "Zeroes" reveal a more introspective and experimental side. The Impact of "In the Meantime" While the entire album is a sonic tour de force, it is the track "In the Meantime" that emerged as Spacehog's signature song. Its infectious, sing-along chorus and memorable riff made it an instant classic, earning it a place in the pantheon of '90s alternative rock anthems. The song's enduring popularity, coupled with its placement in various films, TV shows, and commercials over the years, helped to solidify Spacehog's presence in popular culture, further fueling the collector's fervor for "Resident Alien." Resident Alien's Delayed Recognition The delayed recognition of "Resident Alien" can be attributed to a confluence of factors. In the midst of the '90s alternative rock explosion, many phenomenal albums vied for attention, creating a competitive musical landscape. Additionally, Spacehog's eclectic sound, while endearing to a dedicated fan base, may have posed a challenge in terms of fitting neatly into the dominant musical narratives of the time. As a result, it took time for the album's unique brilliance to find its place in the broader musical conversation. Conclusion: "Resident Alien" as a Collector's Treasure Today, "Resident Alien" stands as a testament to the enduring power of music to transcend eras and genres. Its status as a collector's item is a tribute to the album's lasting impact on those who have discovered its magic. With its infectious melodies, inventive musicianship, and Royston Langdon's distinctive vocals, "Resident Alien" remains a cherished gem for those who value musical authenticity and innovation. As Spacehog's magnum opus, it continues to inspire new generations of music enthusiasts, solidifying its place in the pantheon of alternative rock classics.
